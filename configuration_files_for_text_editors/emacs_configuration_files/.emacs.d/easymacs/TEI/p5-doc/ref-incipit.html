TEI P5: Guidelines for Electronic Text Encoding and Interchange

<incipit>

incipitcontains the incipit of a manuscript item, that is the opening words of the text proper, exclusive of any rubric which might precede it, of sufficient length to identify the work uniquely; such incipts were, in fomer times, frequently used a means of reference to a work, in place of a title.
Attributes: [att.typed att.msExcerpt ]
Classmodel.msItemPart att.typed att.msExcerpt
Declaration
element incipit
{
   att.global.attributes,
   att.typed.attributes,
   att.msExcerpt.attributes,
   macro.phraseSeq
}
Example
 <incipit>Pater noster qui es in celis</incipit>
 <incipit defective="true">tatem dedit hominibus alleluia.</incipit>
 <incipit type="biblical">Ghif ons huden onse dagelix broet</incipit>
 <incipit>O ongehoerde gewerdighe christi</incipit>
 <incipit type="lemma">Firmiter</incipit>
 <incipit>Ideo dicit firmiter quia ordo fidei nostre probari non potest</incipit>
